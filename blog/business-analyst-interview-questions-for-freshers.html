Business Analyst Interview Questions and Answers for Freshers


Business Analyst Interview Questions and Answers

Below are 20 commonly asked Business Analyst interview questions with simple, concise answers you can review quickly.

Que 1. What does a Business Analyst (BA) do?

Answer: A BA bridges business and technology. They elicit and document requirements, analyze processes and data, define solutions, align stakeholders, and ensure the delivered product meets business goals.

Que 2. What is the difference between business requirements and functional requirements?

Answer: Business requirements state the “why/what” at a high level (objectives, outcomes). Functional requirements specify the “how” a system must behave (features, inputs, outputs, rules).

Que 3. How do you elicit requirements from stakeholders?

Answer: Use a mix of techniques: one-on-one interviews, workshops, observation/job shadowing, surveys, document analysis, prototyping, and brainstorming. Choose the method based on stakeholder availability and problem context.

Que 4. What is a BRD and how is it different from an SRS?

Answer: A Business Requirements Document (BRD) captures business needs, scope, and value. A Software Requirements Specification (SRS) details functional and non-functional system requirements used by designers and developers.

Que 5. Explain user stories and acceptance criteria.

Answer: A user story expresses a need from a user’s view (As a <role>, I want <capability> so that <benefit>). Acceptance criteria are the testable conditions that must be satisfied for the story to be considered done.

Que 6. What is a use case? When would you prefer it over user stories?

Answer: A use case describes interactions between an actor and a system to achieve a goal. Prefer use cases when you need detailed flows, alternate paths, and precise system interactions beyond short user stories.

Que 7. How do you prioritize requirements?

Answer: Apply frameworks like MoSCoW (Must/Should/Could/Won’t), value vs. effort matrices, WSJF (for Agile), or cost–benefit analysis. Consider business value, risk, dependencies, and regulatory impact.

Que 8. What is a requirements traceability matrix (RTM)?

Answer: An RTM maps each requirement to its source, design, build item, test case, and release. It ensures coverage, helps impact analysis for changes, and reduces the risk of missed requirements.

Que 9. Explain the difference between Agile and Waterfall for a BA.

Answer: In Waterfall, the BA completes detailed requirements upfront. In Agile, requirements are iterative and incremental; the BA refines backlog items continuously, collaborates closely with the team, and adapts to change.

Que 10. What are common Agile ceremonies a BA participates in?

Answer: Backlog refinement, sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives. The BA clarifies requirements, defines acceptance criteria, and supports demo and feedback loops.

Que 11. How do you manage scope creep?

Answer: Keep a clear baseline, use change control, assess impact (time, cost, risk), get approvals, and reprioritize the backlog. Communicate trade-offs transparently with stakeholders.

Que 12. What non-functional requirements (NFRs) do you commonly capture?

Answer: Performance, security, usability, reliability/availability, scalability, compatibility, accessibility, maintainability, and compliance. NFRs are measurable and tested like functional ones.

Que 13. How do you approach process analysis (AS-IS/TO-BE)?

Answer: Map current (AS-IS) workflows using tools like flowcharts/BPMN, find pain points and waste, design the future (TO-BE) process, validate with stakeholders, define KPIs, and plan change management.

Que 14. What diagrams do BAs commonly create?

Answer: Process flowcharts, BPMN diagrams, use case diagrams, sequence/activity diagrams, context diagrams, ERDs (data models), and wireframes for UI concepts.

Que 15. How do you ensure data quality in analytics projects?

Answer: Define quality dimensions (accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, uniqueness), set validation rules, profile data, document lineage, and implement monitoring and stewardship.

Que 16. What SQL skills are useful for a BA?

Answer: Selecting/filtering (SELECT, WHERE), joining tables, aggregations (GROUP BY), window functions (optional), and basic data validation queries to support analysis and testing.

Que 17. How do you support UAT (User Acceptance Testing)?

Answer: Define acceptance criteria, prepare UAT test scenarios and data, train users, coordinate execution, log defects, verify fixes, and obtain formal sign-off.

Que 18. What is the role of a BA in stakeholder management?

Answer: Identify stakeholders, analyze influence/interest, plan engagement, communicate proactively, resolve conflicts, and maintain alignment on goals, scope, and priorities.

Que 19. How do you quantify business value for a feature?

Answer: Use KPIs and metrics such as revenue uplift, cost savings, risk reduction, compliance impact, and customer experience improvements. Build a simple cost–benefit or ROI estimate to support decisions.

Que 20. What tools do BAs often use?

Answer: Backlog/requirements tools (Jira, Azure Boards), documentation/wikis (Confluence), diagramming (Visio, Draw.io), prototyping (Figma, Balsamiq), data/query tools (Excel, SQL clients), and testing trackers.
